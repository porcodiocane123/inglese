Introduction

In the 1980s, the technologies available to people continue to increase. In fact, the quality of life is always higher. Without a doubt, the representative image of the 1980s is the pocket calculator. It, with its comfort, has conquered many people.

Dimension

The size of the pocket calculator was 25 cm in length and 10 cm in width, finally it was about 2 cm thick.

Type of Technology

The pocket calculator had a processor that was powered in this case by a battery, had an LCD display, i.e. liquid crystal display or had a VFD display, i.e. a bright display, in 1980 the calculator was defined as a mini computer, given that the his name was Computer Calculator.

Date of Invention

In reality the first calculator was created in the early 1960s. Pocket-sized devices became available in the 1970s, but the problem of these things was the battery life, because Led panel wasted a bunch of battery. Later, in the 1980s, the smartest calculator ever was introduced, that could resist enough time to be used independently.

Purpose of Use

It was used from math calculation to a daily use.

Price and Popularity

The pocket calculator, although it is a very comfortable object, is a device that, sooner or later, is destined to become extinct. The increasingly accelerated arrival of modern digital telephones, in fact, is slowly leading disappearance. Despite this, years ago, especially in the 80s, it was widely used by everyone, given its value: from young students to ordinary people. Its price, in fact, varied between 20 and 30 euros which, converted to the currency of the time, corresponded to 38725 lire.

Age of Those Use it and Time it Was Used

The pocket calculator was used extensively in high school by kids who chose a more math-based school. Others who used it were families to do quick calculations since normal calculators were much larger and more difficult to use.

Color

It had very classic colors like black, white and grey. Very colorful things were not appreciated at the time, so those colors were not used.
